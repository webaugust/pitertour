St. Petersburg City Break
St Petersburg is the second largest city of Russia, major sea and river port. With 86 canals and over 45 islands St Petersburg is often called Venice of the North. There are over 580 bridges, which make up an essential part of the city infrastructure. Many of the bridges are engineering and architectural masterpieces and harmoniously contribute to the incomparable image of the city. Created by Emperor Peter the Great in 1703 to be his “window on Europe”, St Petersburg combines the fascinating Russian heritage with a distinctly European outlook. A city of palaces and museums, broad avenues and winding canals, St. Petersburg has gained the reputation of one of the Great Cities of Europe. Altogether 4,000 outstanding architectural, historical and cultural monuments are on the UNESCO World Heritage List.

Moscow City Break
Moscow, the capital of the Russian Federation, the seat of Russia’s president and the federal government, is one of the biggest megapolises in the world with the population exceeding 10 million. As Russia’s principle city and one of the oldest Russian settlements, set up in 1147, Moscow played a prominent role in Russia’s history, which is reflected in numerous monuments, such as magnificent Kremlin, lovely St Basil’s Cathedral, ancient monasteries and splendid manors. Influenced by both Byzantine and Western trends, the city’s architectural image is an eclectic mixture of Oriental and European styles, of ancient and modern constructions, which makes up a unique feature of Moscow as a city of great historical and cultural traditions.
